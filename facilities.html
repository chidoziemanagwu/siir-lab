Shared UA Facilities

​The SIIR-Lab collaborates with and utilizes resources available in Research Centers affiliated with the College of Engineering and Culverhouse College of Business. Description of and links to select Centers are provided below. ​

​Alabama Industrial Assessment Center

This center engages in energy-saving practices and assist manufacturers with energy conservation, waste reduction, and productivity increases (https://iac.ua.edu)

​Center for Advanced Public Safety

This center is dedicated to the implementation and utilization of cutting-edge technologies to enhance public safety and well-being of citizens of the world (https://www.caps.ua.edu)

Integrative Center for Athletic and Sport Technology

I-CAST offers a unifying application for technologies to improve performance of competitive and non-competitive athletes (http://icast.eng.ua.edu)

Center for Sustainable Infrastructure

This center conducts research associated with expanding, maintaining and rehabilitating all aspects of physical infrastructure. The center is particularly focused on the creation of resilient infrastructure, including assessment of infrastructure conditions, vulnerability and recovery from disaster (http://csi.eng.ua.edu)

University Transportation Center for Alabama

This center seeks to advance technology and expertise in the multiple disciplines that comprise transportation through the mechanisms of education, research, and technology transfer while serving as a center of excellence (http://utca.eng.ua.edu)

Center for Business and Economic Research

The Center for Business and Economic Research (CBER) was established in 1930 as an outreach unit within the Culverhouse College of Business at the University of Alabama to promote Alabama’s economic development by providing insight (data and analysis) through research and public service. (https://cber.culverhouse.ua.edu)
